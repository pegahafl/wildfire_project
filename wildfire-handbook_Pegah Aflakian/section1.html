Wildfire spread depends on a combination of factors that interact dynamically. Weather conditions such as drought, heat, and wind dry out vegetation, making fuels easier to ignite. Once a fire is burning, these same factors increase its intensity. Topography also plays a major role, as fire spreads quickly uphill and more slowly downhill.
